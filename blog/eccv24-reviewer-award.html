Recognition as Outstanding Reviewer

I am glad to announce that this week I have been selected as one of the Outstanding Reviewers out of 7,293 reviewers at the 2024 European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV)!

This was my first time serving as a reviewer for ECCV. I am grateful to the organizing committee for the invitation and honored by this recognition from the community.

üéì Double Recognition in 2024:
This award follows the Outstanding Reviewer recognition I received at the 2024 IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) in June, where I was also a first-time reviewer. It's gratifying to receive this recognition from both major conferences in the same year.

The Value of Quality Peer Review

I believe that writing high-quality reviews and providing constructive feedback is a fundamental service to our research community. Quality peer review helps ensure that disseminated research is relevant, impactful, and clearly communicated to readers.

The review process serves two important functions:

  • For accepted papers: Clear acknowledgment and constructive feedback help authors strengthen their contributions for publication and presentation.
  • For papers not accepted: Most of the cases, papers are not yet ready for publication and detailed feedback should clearly signal areas for improvement while recognizing the work's strengths, giving authors a path forward.

Congratulations to the Community

I am grateful for the opportunity to have contributed to both CVPR and ECCV in a positive way. I extend my congratulations to all the other Outstanding Reviewers recognized at these conferences: your dedication to quality review strengthens our entire field.

To the authors whose papers were not accepted: I hope the feedback you received is an opportunity to learn and grow and helps you improve your work for future submissions.

About This Recognition

This post celebrates the outstanding reviewer recognition at ECCV 2024 and reflects on the importance of quality peer review in advancing the computer vision community. The recognition highlights the value of constructive feedback in the academic publishing process.
