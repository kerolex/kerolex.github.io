Alessio is a researcher with 10 years' experience and expertise in computer vision and deep learning. Alessio contributed computer vision and deep learning methods and audio-visual datasets for human-robot collaborations, decentralised vision methods for collaborative 3D reconstruction and navigation, and benchmarks of graph neural networks for visual privacy and explainability. As a Postdoctoral Research Assistant with the School of Electronic Engineering and Computer Science at Queen Mary University of London (QMUL), UK, he is currently investigating vision-language models for zero-shot open-vocabulary object detection, learning-based models for visual privacy (image privacy classification), and perception-based pipelines for human-to-robot handovers.

Alessio obtained the Ph.D. in Electronic Engineering with a research focus on Computer Vision from Queen Mary University of London, UK, in September 2020. Alessio obtained the Master Degree in Telecommunications Engineering and the Bachelor Degree in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering from the University of Trento in March 2015 and February 2012, respectively.
